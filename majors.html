Majors

Business Administration

Degrees Awarded (2023): 276

The General Business Administration & Management major is the most common bachelor's degree concentration at TCNJ. This program provides a comprehensive foundation in business principles, preparing graduates for various roles in the corporate world.

Biological Sciences

Degrees Awarded (2023): 137

TCNJ's Biological Sciences program is highly competitive, with over 1,200 applications for approximately 100 spots. This major offers tracks in general biology, secondary education, and dual-degree pre-professional programs, including pathways to medicine, optometry, pharmacy, and physical therapy.

Psychology

Degrees Awarded (2023): 134

The Psychology major at TCNJ provides students with a deep understanding of human behavior and mental processes. Graduates often pursue careers in counseling, social services, or continue their education in graduate programs.


Biology (B.A. / B.S.)

Degrees Awarded (2023): 126

Offers tracks in general biology, secondary education, and dual-degree options (like medicine, pharmacy, physical therapy). Hands-on labs and research opportunities with faculty are a big focus.

Computer Science (B.S.)

Degrees Awarded (2023): 115

Covers programming, software engineering, algorithms, cybersecurity, and data science. Students can take part in research, internships, and even collaborate with tech companies.

Music Education (B.M.)

Degrees Awarded (2023): 100

Prepares students to become certified K–12 music teachers. Combines music performance, conducting, and pedagogy courses with real classroom experience. Alumni often go on to teaching, performance, or graduate study in music.

Learn More

At The College of New Jersey (TCNJ), majors are designed to give students both a strong foundation in the liberal arts and in-depth knowledge of their chosen field. Students usually declare a major when applying, but many have the flexibility to explore courses during their first year before making a final decision. Each major combines core classes, specialized courses, and often hands-on experiences like research, internships, or fieldwork. Many programs also allow dual majors, minors, or interdisciplinary tracks, so students can tailor their education to fit their career goals. In addition, TCNJ emphasizes close collaboration with faculty, giving undergraduates opportunities that are often reserved for graduate students at larger universities. Small class sizes mean students get personalized guidance and build strong academic relationships. Many majors include a capstone project or senior thesis, where students apply what they’ve learned to a real-world problem or research question. Graduates leave TCNJ well-prepared for careers, graduate school, or professional programs thanks to this well-rounded academic structure.
